Why this matters

Midband/HF distortion = fatigue

Non‑linear distortion blurs vocals and cymbal detail and leads to listening fatigue. A big contributor is current distortion from inductance modulation in the driver.

We control what makes sound

Electrodynamic drivers create sound from coil current (Lorentz force), not strictly coil voltage. So we improve current linearity where you hear it most.

Clarity without pricier drivers

Reducing current‑driven nonlinearity drops 3rd & 5th harmonics to levels you’d expect from far more expensive drivers. That’s clarity you can hear.

How it works in the real world

Resonance stays controlled

Pure current drive removes electrical damping (Qes), lifting total Q near resonance. Our Band‑Selective Current Drive keeps low output impedance at low frequencies so the amp behaves like a voltage amp around Fs—your woofer remains well‑damped.

Current control where it matters

Above the LF region, the module presents high output impedance so coil current is less sensitive to the driver’s back‑EMF nonlinearity—cleaning up the 3rd/5th harmonics that cloud detail.

Set once with a simple switch

Use the Transition Frequency switch to place your driver’s Fs inside the low‑impedance region (e.g., Low • Mid • High). Choose once during setup—then forget it.

FAQ

Does it include DSP for crossovers and EQ?
Yes. The module has a built‑in DSP controlled by a desktop app. Implement your active crossover and EQ in software—no passive network sits between the amp and the driver.
Does it work with passive crossovers?
For current control to be effective, the amplifier should connect directly to the driver. Use the built‑in DSP to implement your crossover and EQ actively.
Will it over‑excite my woofer at resonance?
No—Band‑Selective Current Drive keeps output impedance low at low frequencies so the amp behaves like a voltage amp around resonance, preserving damping.
Is this adaptive?
No. The profile is fixed but frequency‑shaped. You select the transition frequency during setup so your driver’s Fs sits in the low‑impedance region. After that, it’s hands‑off.
Do I need to be “super technical” to use it?
The desktop app exposes friendly presets and simple controls. You can go deep if you want, but you don’t have to.

Technical appendix (optional reading)
